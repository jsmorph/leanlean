## Current Reading

This does not look like a single stuck recursive cycle.  The profiler changes phase, heartbeats continue, and counters keep rising across `defEq`, WHNF, projection reduction, inference, and checking.  A literal loop would more likely show a fixed mark, no phase transition, or repeated work with no growth in surrounding counters.

It does show repeated exact conversion work.  In the 10,000,000-step run, 130,862 of 134,977 non-alpha `defEq` observations were repeat hits, and nearly all of those repeat hits were successful conversions.  That is strong evidence for a reusable success layer or a more principled normalization path for algebra-instance projection chains, but the earlier exact-key memo experiment warns that a naive cache can cost more than it saves.

The repeated terms point back to inherited algebra structures and pointwise instances.  Projection-target WHNF already helped by preserving normalized projection targets, but the checker still revisits equal structure-conversion paths many times.

## Post-WHNF Alpha Experiment

The repeated successful goals made the earlier post-WHNF alpha experiment worth retesting.  The change adds an alpha-equivalence check after both sides of structural conversion reach WHNF.  This catches comparisons such as a beta-reduced lambda application against the same bound variable, and it avoids descending through some already-normalized structure paths.

## Success-Only DefEq Cache

The outcome data justified a narrower cache experiment than the earlier memoization attempt.  `mpc-dynamic-profile-export --defeq-success-cache` stores only successful non-alpha `defEq` goals, keyed by level-parameter context, local context, and both expressions.  It does not cache failures, and it does not cache WHNF results.

Three 1,000,000-budget runs checked declaration 12,736 before exhausting the budget.  They produced the same counted result: `checked`, 716,661 steps, 27,548 defEq calls, 5,694 structural calls, 292,438 WHNF calls, 48,489 projection reductions, 18,620 definition unfolds, 4,232 success-cache hits, 5,694 misses, 5,679 entries, and no dropped inserts.  Selected-declaration time was 831 ms in the first run, 784 ms in the rerun, and 766 ms after adding the full-replay hook.

This is the first diagnostic optimization that turns the selected declaration from budget-exhausted into checked.  It also confirms that the repeated successful conversions are not incidental telemetry noise.  The cache remains outside `MPC.Check`; the experiment belongs to the replay adapter and selected-declaration profiler.
